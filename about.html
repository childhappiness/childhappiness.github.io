About us

Vision

To create a sustainable, vibrant centre of excellence in teaching, learning, and community service in the slum region and beyond.

Mission

To make a child happy through basic informal and formal education rehabilitative initiatives for vulnerable and out-of-school children in view of building a sustainable community with improved standards of living. The main thrust is to enhance their opportunities to realize their full potential and capabilities.

Secretariat and Training Centre

The head office is based in Makerere Kavule, a Kampala suburb off Bombo Road, headed by the director. The secretariat is responsible for the day-to-day implementation of policy and programs of CHEC. The director is supported by program coordinators, instructors and professional teachers who train vulnerable and out-of-school children in various skills offered by the program at CHEC. CHEC may, from time to time, require volunteers to perform a wide variety of functions, but especially in training and social work.

Principles Of Conduct

CHEC believes in upholding its constitution that clearly defines issues on membership, sources of funding, networking, and more.
CHEC beleives in self-help participation and identifying one’s potential.
CHEC believes that every human being must live a good quality life.
CHEC beleives in strict accountability of all resources, funds, materials, and time.
CHEC works to unite staff towards acheiving the mission by providing an attractive working environment and helping them in developing their careers and personal talents, as well as security of employment.
CHEC works within governmental policies to achieve goals that affect the life of both vulnerable, and out-of-school children.
Any gifts or donations which improve the status of our organization are welcome.

Ownership, Governance, and Leadership

The policy framework of CHEC is guided by a draft memorandum and articles of association awaiting registration by the NGO board of Uganda.

Program Focus and Coverage

CHEC’s geographical coverage is currently the Kawempe division of Kampala district; however, through child advocacy and community outreach, we may later go beyond Kampala district into nearby Wakiso district to the north.

The direct beneficiaries of CHEC programmes are vulnerable children, out-of-school children, and any other children from the community that might need quality education services. CHEC’s activities are educational and rehabilitative in nature, including:

  • Reaching out to vulnerable and out-of-school children in their homes, and through local community sessions, family tracing, and community sensitization.
  • Training the vulnerable and out-of-school children who are of school-going age in literacy skills, equipping them with rehabilitative abilities, and developing their budding talents and capabilities.
  • Co-ordinating and supporting campaigns and actions on child education at the community level.
  • Active research and surveys on specific issues affecting the out-of-school youth.
  • Publication of children/youth experiences.
  • Education support through providing free education and stationery for vulnerable children both at kindergarten and primary-school levels in CHEC’s operational area.
  • Empowering staff members with business management skills and self-help projects.

Distinctive Competencies

CHEC has excelled in the areas of child frame recovery, counseling and resettlement, as well as community mobilization, and has suffiecient experience and potential to undertake the types of projects we have chosen to. We are continually hiring personnel and acquiring the necessary resources to ensure success.

Achievements to date

We have successfully completed five years of operation offering early formal education (literacy, numeracy, and life skills) to children at kindergarten level, and started grassroots establishments for the lower primary levels.

CHEC uses two languages during instruction, i.e. local and English, to enable the trainers to pass on skills. Additionally, instruction charts and other designed materials are used to facilitate the process.

CHEC integrates life and coping skills into the training programmes, as well as incorporating music as a medium of expression for ideas.
